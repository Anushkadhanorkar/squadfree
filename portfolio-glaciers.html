How global warming has affected glaciers

Due to global climate change, Antarctica is experiencing changes in air and ocean temperatures. As a result, some glaciers are showing increased flow speeds, thinning, and retreat. As the glaciers flow faster and lose more ice to the ocean, they contribute to global sea-level rise. Antarctica's remoteness and harsh conditions make research challenging.
