How pollution has affected this river

The Amazon Rainforest acts as a significant carbon sink, capturing large quantities of CO2 from the atmosphere. However, deforestation for agriculture, mining, and infrastructure projects has reduced the size of the rainforest, compromising its ability to sequester carbon. Increased temperatures and changing rainfall patterns can make the Amazon more susceptible to fires. The Amazon plays a crucial role in regulating the world's climate by absorbing large amounts of CO2. Significant changes or degradation to this ecosystem can accelerate global warming and disrupt weather patterns globally.
